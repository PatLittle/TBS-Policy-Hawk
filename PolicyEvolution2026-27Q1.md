## 2026-04-30 — Directive on the Management of Materiel

**Issue:** [#253](https://github.com/PatLittle/TBS-Policy-Hawk/issues/253)

- Prior version used for comparison: `data/Directive/Management of Materiel Directive on the_2025-08-31.xml`

### Summary

This is a targeted amendment to **Appendix A: Mandatory Procedures for Land Vehicle Fleet Management**, not a broad rewrite of the directive. The note to reader identifies the April 30, 2026 change as amending **A.2.3.1.4** and **A.2.3.1.5** to reduce reporting burden, streamline departmental reporting requirements, and align with the Greening Government Strategy.

### Substantive changes identified

| Section | Prior version / effect | New/current version / effect | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Note to reader** | Only described the July 11, 2023 fleet-management amendments. | Adds: **Effective April 30, 2026**, sections **A.2.3.1.4** and **A.2.3.1.5** amended to reduce reporting burden, streamline departmental reporting requirements, and align with the Greening Government Strategy. | Confirms the purpose and location of the issue-triggering amendment. |
| **A.2.3.1.4 – fleet greening / planning** | Prior version had fleet greening and planning controls spread across the Appendix A planning requirements. | New version consolidates this into a broader requirement to support the Greening Government Strategy’s conventional fleet commitments, including internal policies and controls to prioritize ZEVs, right-size fleets and vehicles, promote energy-efficient operating practices, and long-term strategic planning for fleet greening, including requirements for the national safety and security fleet. | Reframes the planning obligation around the current Greening Government Strategy rather than a more detailed reporting/process requirement. |
| **A.2.3.1.5** | The old sequence included an additional planning/reporting-related requirement between A.2.3.1.4 and A.2.3.1.6. | In the current version, the sequence moves from **A.2.3.1.4** directly to **A.2.3.1.6**. | Indicates the separate A.2.3.1.5 requirement was removed or consolidated into A.2.3.1.4, consistent with reducing reporting burden. |

### Practical effect

The amendment mainly affects fleet reporting and planning administration:

1. **Less standalone reporting burden:** one planning/reporting requirement appears removed or merged.
2. **More direct GGS alignment:** the surviving A.2.3.1.4 is framed around the Greening Government Strategy’s conventional fleet commitments.
3. **ZEV and fleet-right-sizing emphasis retained:** the directive still emphasizes prioritizing zero-emission vehicles, right-sizing fleets and vehicles, energy-efficient operations, and long-term fleet greening.
4. **Core data obligations retained:** the fleet-management system and mandatory fleet data fields remain in place, so this is not a removal of fleet data collection generally.

### Watch item

Because the note says the change is to reduce reporting burden and streamline reporting, it may be worth checking whether any removed reporting detail was moved into the Centre for Greening Government Inventory process, implementation guidance, or the Greening Government Strategy rather than eliminated entirely.

| Section | Prior version | New/current version | Interpretation |

The amendment mainly affects departmental reporting operations:

2. **Later annual deadline:** the annual deadline shifts from **May 30** to **September 30**.
3. **Reporting burden reduction:** separate reporting requirements for former public servant pension-related contracts and emergency contracts appear to have been removed from Appendix C.
4. **No broad rewrite:** the main procurement framework, roles, contracting limits, Indigenous procurement, Buy Canadian, trade agreement, and socio-economic/environmental requirements appear otherwise largely unchanged in the sampled comparison.

### Watch item

Because the amendment removes or consolidates reporting obligations, it may be worth checking whether those reporting requirements were moved into another instrument, guide, or operational process rather than fully eliminated.

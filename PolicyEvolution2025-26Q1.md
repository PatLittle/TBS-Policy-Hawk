# Policy Evolution Report (2026-04-01 to 2026-06-30)

This document summarizes substantive policy, directive, and guideline changes detected and analyzed during Q1 FY 2026-27.

---

## 2026-04-27 — Guidelines on Making Communications Products and Activities Accessible (Issue #252)

### Summary
Targeted maintenance update to accessibility references and wording rather than a broad rewrite.

### Key Changes
- Replaced references to the Guideline on Making Information Technology Usable by All with references to the Accessible Canada Regulations and associated guidance.
- Updated font guidance from "easy to read" to fonts that can be resized without losing clarity and readability.
- Clarified decorative image guidance by explicitly specifying null alt text (`alt=""`).
- WCAG reference changed from a WCAG 2.1 reference URL to a WCAG 2.0 reference.

### Practical Effect
- Better alignment with the current Accessible Canada regulatory framework.
- More precise implementation guidance for accessible content creators.
- Possible need to verify whether the WCAG reference change was intentional.

## 2026-04-30 — Directive on the Management of Materiel (Issue #253)

### Summary
Targeted amendment to Appendix A: Mandatory Procedures for Land Vehicle Fleet Management.

### Key Changes
- Sections A.2.3.1.4 and A.2.3.1.5 amended to reduce reporting burden and align with the Greening Government Strategy.
- Fleet greening requirements consolidated around:
  - prioritizing zero-emission vehicles (ZEVs);
  - right-sizing fleets;
  - energy-efficient operations; and
  - long-term fleet greening planning.
- One planning/reporting requirement appears to have been removed or consolidated.

### Practical Effect
- Reduced administrative reporting burden.
- Stronger alignment with the Greening Government Strategy.
- Fleet data collection and fleet management system requirements remain intact.

---

## 2026-04-30 — Directive on the Management of Procurement (Issue #254)

### Summary
Amendment focused on reducing reporting burden and streamlining departmental reporting requirements.

### Key Changes
- Annual reporting moved from calendar-year reporting to fiscal-year reporting.
- Reporting deadlines changed from May 30 to September 30.
- Reporting requirements concerning former public servants in receipt of pensions were removed.
- Reporting requirements concerning emergency contracts and contractual arrangements were removed.

### Practical Effect
- Better alignment with government fiscal reporting cycles.
- Reduced duplicate or standalone reporting requirements.
- Procurement operational framework remains largely unchanged.

---

## 2026-06-01 — Policy on the Planning and Management of Investments (Issue #255)

### Summary
Targeted amendment affecting procurement authorities and approval mechanisms.

### Key Changes
- Added authority for the President of the Treasury Board to issue, amend, or rescind contracting limits in Appendix A of the Directive on the Management of Procurement when proposed by the Secretary of the Treasury Board and affecting multiple departments.
- Changed approval authority for government-wide procurement-leveraged socio-economic and environmental programs from Treasury Board approval to President of the Treasury Board approval.
- Added exception where Cabinet approval already exists.

### Practical Effect
- Streamlines approval and maintenance of procurement authorities.
- Delegates more operational authority to the President of the Treasury Board.
- Reduces the need for full Treasury Board approvals in certain circumstances.

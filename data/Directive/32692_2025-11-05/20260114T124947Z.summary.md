## High-Level Takeaways
*   **New Appendix F (Professional Services) Implemented:** The policy now incorporates mandatory procedures for Business Owners when procuring professional services valued over $\$40,000$, effective September 30, 2024. This introduces requirements for alignment with HR resourcing strategies, confirmation of inability to staff positions, and specific acknowledgement requirements upon contract signing.
*   **Update to Indigenous Procurement Reporting (Appendix E):** Reporting requirements for the 5% Indigenous procurement target have been updated regarding the submission and publication timelines relative to fiscal year-end.
*   **Update to Proactive Publication (Appendix C):** Requirements for establishing risk-based internal controls for proactive publication of contracts over $\$10,000$ have been formally integrated under the responsibility of Senior Designated Officials.
*   **New Appendix D.7 (Buy Canadian):** The Buy Canadian Procurement Policy Framework is now referenced as a mandatory element under Appendix D, reflecting its expected integration by December 16, 2025 (noted in the preamble).

## Clause-Level Changes
- **Note to reader (Effective May 29, 2024):** The note now explicitly mentions the inclusion of new requirements related to values and ethics, documentation, and reporting via subsections 4.1.7, 4.2.2, 4.2.3.4, 4.2.3.5, 4.3.2.2, 4.10.1.10, and 5.6.9, as well as Appendix D.6 and Appendix F.
- **Section 4.1.2 (Framework Requirements):** New clauses 4.1.2.15 and 4.1.2.16 were added to require the framework to include risk-based systems of internal control for procurement transactions and for ensuring the accuracy/timeliness of proactive publishing for contracts over $\$10,000$, respectively.
- **Section 4.1.7 (Reporting on Per Diem Services):** This subsection, related to reporting on high-value per diem professional services contracts, is newly included under the responsibilities of Senior Designated Officials.
- **Section 4.2.2 (Business Owners - Values and Ethics):** This subsection now explicitly references adherence to the *Values and Ethics Code for the Public Sector* and the *Directive on Conflict of Interest*.
- **Section 4.2.3 (Business Owners - Planning Support):** New clauses 4.2.3.4 and 4.2.3.5 were added, requiring the development of clear SOW/evaluation criteria and adherence to the new Appendix F for professional services.
- **Section 4.3.2.2 (Contracting Authorities - Integrity):** This subsection now explicitly requires monitoring, preventing, identifying, and reporting conflicts of interest.
- **Section 5.6.9 (PSPC Role):** This subsection, referencing the administration of the *Ineligibility and Suspension Policy*, is now explicitly referenced in the May 29, 2024, amendment note.
- **Section 10.1.10 (Contracting Authorities - Records):** This subsection requires documentation of the name and title of the assigned business owner.
- **Appendix A.2 (Exceptional Limits):** Several departments (e.g., CSIS, Correctional Service Canada, Global Affairs Canada, RCMP) now have specific clauses referencing contracts "in support of sensitive operations," which may utilize previously undefined exceptional limits.
- **Appendix C.2.3 (Internal Controls):** Senior Designated Officials are now explicitly responsible for establishing risk-based internal controls for ensuring the accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of proactive publication for contracts over $\$10,000$.
- **Appendix C.2.5.1 (Proactive Publication):** The reporting timeframe for quarterly publication has been slightly modified: 30 days after the end of the first three quarters, and 60 days after the end of the fourth quarter.
- **Appendix D.5 (Code of Conduct):** Clarification added that PSPC develops/updates the Code in consultation with TBS.
- **Appendix D.6 (Ineligibility and Suspension Policy):** This policy is now explicitly referenced under PSPC's responsibilities.
- **Appendix D.7 (Buy Canadian):** This section is newly included, detailing responsibilities for implementing the Buy Canadian Procurement Policy Framework for SDOs, CAs, and PSPC.
- **Appendix E.2.2.1.2 & E.2.2.3.4 (Indigenous Reporting):** Reporting submission deadlines for the mandatory minimum target plans (E.2.2.1.2) are by March 31 for the following fiscal year. Performance reports (E.2.2.3.4) must be submitted to ISC within six months after fiscal year-end.
- **Appendix E.3.5 (ISC Roles):** ISC is now responsible for prescribing the schedule for departments to meet the 5% target (E.3.5.1) and issuing the annual call letter for performance reports (E.3.5.3).
- **Appendix E.4 (Enquiries):** Contact emails for ISC and PSPC have been updated from masked email links to plaintext addresses.
- **Appendix F (New):** A completely new appendix detailing Mandatory Procedures for Business Owners When Procuring Professional Services, effective September 30, 2024.

## Notable Additions
*   **Appendix F: Mandatory Procedures for Business Owners When Procuring Professional Services:** Establishes specific governance and documentation requirements for business owners when contracting for professional services over $\$40,000$, focusing on alignment with HR staffing, knowledge transfer, and conflict of interest acknowledgement.
*   **Section 4.1.7:** New reporting requirement for SDOs regarding high-value per diem professional services contracts.
*   **Section 4.1.2.15 & 4.1.2.16:** Inclusion of mandatory internal controls related to general procurement transactions and specific controls for proactive publication accuracy (over $\$10,000$).
*   **Appendix D.7:** Introduction of the Buy Canadian Procurement Policy Framework implementation requirements.

## Notable Removals
*   No substantive sections or clauses appear to have been removed entirely, though the context of previous amendments has been incorporated into the structure of the 'Note to reader' section.

## Potential Impacts
*   **Increased Business Owner Responsibility:** Appendix F significantly increases the governance burden on business owners for professional services procurements, requiring formal justification that requirements cannot be met through internal staffing and specific confirmation of review/non-involvement in solicitation processes.
*   **Enhanced Internal Control over Disclosure:** New requirements in sections 4.1.2.16 and Appendix C.2.3 mandate specific risk-based internal controls for the accuracy and timeliness of contract publication over $\$10,000$, necessitating immediate procedural updates.
*   **Timeline Alignment for Indigenous Procurement:** Departments must align their procurement planning submissions to ISC by March 31 for the subsequent fiscal year, and final performance reports are due six months post-year-end.

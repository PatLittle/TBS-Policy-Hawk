Date: July 1, 2011

To: Functional Heads of Human Resources, Financial and Administration Services, Chairs of Northern Federal Councils

The National Joint Council Isolated Posts and Government Housing Committee has recently approved the designation of Kegaska, Quebec as a new isolated posts under the IPGHD. All allowances and benefits for this location will be effective July 1, 2011.

In addition, the results of the Statistics Canada survey on the Living Costs Differential (LCD) Indexes for the Nunavut Region conducted in October 2010 were recently communicated to the National Joint Council Isolated Posts and Government Housing Committee (NJC-IPGHD). A total of twenty-five (25) isolated posts (fourteen (14) posts in the Baffin Region of Nunavut, two (2) posts in the Ungava Bay Region of northern Quebec, eight (8) posts in the Kivillaq Region of Nunavut and one (1) post in the Northern Manitoba) were involved in this review and the results can be summarized as follows:

## No change in LCD level - 15 posts

- Arctic Bay, NU
- Cape Dorset, NU
- Clyder River, NU
- Eureka, NU
- Hall Beach, NU
- Igloolik, NU
- Iqaluit, NU
- Kimmirut, NU
- Pangnirtung, NU
- Pond Inlet, NU
- Sanikiluaq, NU
- Coral Harbour, NU
- Kuujjuak (Que)
- Quaqtaq (Que)
- Churchill (Man)

## Increase by one LCD level - 1 posts

- Qikiqtarjuaq, NU

## Decrease by one LCD level - 9 posts

- Grise Fiord, NU
- Resolute, NU
- Arviat, NU
- Baker Lake, NU
- Chesterfield Inlet, NU
- Kugaaruk (Pelly Bay), NU
- Rankin Inlet, NU
- Repulse Bay, NU
- Whale Cove, NU

These changes will be effective July 1, 2011 for the Isolated Post which had a classification increase in the LCD level as per 2.11.2 of the NJC Isolated Posts and Government Housing Directive (IPGHD). Please note that for the nine (9) locations which decreased, notification must be provided to employees as the effective date of the changes will be the first day of the fourth calendar month after the month in which written notification was received as per 2.12.4 a) of the NJC-IPGHD. An email message is acceptable to provide such notification. Details regarding the methodology can be found in the "[Guide to National Joint Council Rates and Allowances](http://www.njc-cnm.gc.ca/doc.php?did=117%20&lang=en)".

Departmental employees or regional staff should direct any questions on this subject to the appropriate departmental corporate official. [View the list of Designated Departmental Contacts](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/psm-fpfm/pay-remuneration/other-autres/ddhc-cmdl-eng.asp).

Claudia Zovatto Senior Director, Union Engagement & NJC Support Compensation and Labour Relations Sector Office of the Chief Human Resources Officer

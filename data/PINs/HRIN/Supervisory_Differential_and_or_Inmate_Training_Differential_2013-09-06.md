# Supervisory Differential and/or Inmate Training Differential

## We have archived this page and will not be updating it.

You can use it for research or reference.

Date: September 6, 2013

To: Heads of Human Resources Directors/Chiefs of Labour/Relations and Compensation

This purpose of this bulletin is to explain the method of calculation to be followed effective immediately in respect of the supervisory differential and/or inmate training differential payable under the following collective agreements: [Air Traffic Control](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pubs_pol/hrpubs/coll_agre/ai/ai-eng.asp) (AI), [Operational Services](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pubs_pol/hrpubs/coll_agre/sv/sv-eng.asp) (SV) and [Non-Supervisory Printing Services](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pubs_pol/hrpubs/coll_agre/pr-ns/pr-ns-eng.asp) (PR-NS).

The supervisory differential and the inmate training differential are derived from a classification decision, and therefore, are part of the level of the position. For this reason, they are to be included in the calculation when determining if an appointment is a promotion or a deployment even though the actual group, sub-group and/or level remains unchanged. They are also included in the calculation to determine the new rate of pay.

However, when determining the “lowest pay increment”, neither the supervisory differential nor the inmate training differential should be included in this calculation.

The new rate of pay would be the one that gives the employee an increase (inclusive of the supervisory differential and/or the inmate training differential) even if the basic rate of pay for the new position is lower than the basic rate of pay of the current position.

An example is provided in [Annex A](https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/information-notice/supervisory-differential-inmate-training-differential.html#a).

Questions pertaining to this bulletin should be directed to your departmental Corporate Compensation or Labour Relations officials who in turn can contact the [Compensation and Labour Relations Interpretations mailbox](mailto:Interpretations@tbs-sct.gc.ca).

## Annex A

### Example of method of calculation for the supervisory differential and/or inmate training differential

(In the example below, the supervisory differential is shown first in parentheses, the inmate training differential second. Rates of pay in effect August 5, 2012)

A GS-FOS-6 (0 + B1) is required to perform the duties of a GS-FOS-7 (B2 + C2).

| Maximum rate of pay in new position: | $27.77 + 1.67 + 3.33 = $32.77 |
| --- | --- |
| Maximum rate of pay in current position: | $26.53 + 0.00 + 1.86 = $28.39 |
| Difference: | $4.38 |
| Lowest pay increment in new position: | $1.11 |

This is considered a promotion pursuant to Part 2, subsection 2.2.3 of the Appendix to the Directive on Terms and Conditions of Employment.

| Employee’s current rate of pay: | $24.40 + 0.00 + 1.71 = $26.11 |
| --- | --- |
| Add lowest pay increment in new position: | + $1.11 |
| New rate of pay must be at least: | $27.22 |

| GS-FOS- 7 | Step 1 | Step 2 | Step 3 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hourly rates of pay | $25.55 | $26.66 | $27.77 |
| Supervisory differential - 6% | + 1.53 | + 1.60 | + 1.67 |
| Inmate training differential - 12% | + 3.07 | + 3.20 | + 3.33 |
| Total | $30.15 | $31.46 | $32.77 |

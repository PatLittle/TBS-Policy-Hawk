# Union Dues Directive

Date: February 24, 2011

To: Heads of Human Resources; Heads of Compensation; Heads of Labour Relations; Chiefs of Staffing

On May 13, 2009, I wrote to you to announce the coming into force of the [Policy on Terms and Conditions of Employment](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=15771) as well as three policy instruments. At that time, I also mentioned that other policy instruments would be issued.

Today, I am pleased to announce the coming into force of the [Directive on Union Dues](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=21104), which takes effect March 1, 2011. This Directive replaces the Union Dues Check-Off Policy. It strengthens departmental accountability. It also clarifies functional specialists’ responsibilities and the processes to be followed.

You will find attached for your information the Directive, a [reference table](http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/lrco-rtor/conditions/reftab-tabref-eng.asp) which will be particularly appreciated by the Compensation community, as well as [Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/labour-management/union-dues/frequently-asked-questions-union-dues.html) that you can share with the Compensation, Staffing and Labour Relations communities.

For more information please contact France Olivier-Paquette, Senior Corporate Pay Administration Advisor, Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat, at [Contact France Olivier-Paquette by E-mail France.Olivier-Paquette@tbs-sct.gc.ca](mailto:France.Olivier-Paquette@tbs-sct.gc.ca) or at 613-952-2978.

Hélène Laurendeau Assistant Deputy Minister Compensation and Labour Relations

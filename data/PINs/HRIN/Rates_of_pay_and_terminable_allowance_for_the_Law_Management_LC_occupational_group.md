# Rates of pay and terminable allowance for the Law Management (LC) occupational group

Date: June 21, 2016

To: Heads of Human Resources Directors/Chiefs of Labour Relations and Compensation

I am pleased to announce the revised rates of pay, as approved by the Treasury Board of Canada, for the Law Management (LC) group.

## Rates of pay

The rates of pay applicable to the LC group can be found online at [Rates of pay for unrepresented and senior excluded employees](https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/pay/rates-pay/rates-pay-unrepresented-senior-excluded-employees.html).

## Terminable allowance

The previous terminable allowance that expired on March 31, 2015, has been reinstated for the LC group levels 1 to 3. The terminable allowance is effective as of April 1, 2015, to March 31, 2016. An incumbent occupying an LC position, receiving an LC rate of pay and performing the duties of the position is eligible for the terminable allowance.

The terminable allowance amounts can be found online at [Rates of pay for unrepresented and senior excluded employees](https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/pay/rates-pay/rates-pay-unrepresented-senior-excluded-employees.html)

Note: Toronto rates of pay will only be used to determine the terminable allowance for employees in the LC group who are located in Toronto and receive Toronto rates of pay.

The terminable allowance will not be included in the calculation of performance pay for eligible employees occupying LC positions.

For employees working less than the normal 37.5 hour work week, the allowance will be prorated based on the number of hours they work compared to a 37.5 hour work week.

Effective April 1, 2016, the terminable allowance will be rolled into the salary of the LC group levels 1 to 3.

## Future Law Management salary adjustments

Effective April 1, 2016, all future LC salary adjustments will be aligned to those provided to the Law Practitioner (LP) group for the LC group levels 1 to 3 (LC-01 to LC-03) based on the Law Practitioner to Law Management equivalency table that was established when the LC group was created.

# Contracting Policy Notice 2020-2: Replacement of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA)

From [Treasury Board Secretariat](https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat.html)

Date: June 30, 2020

To: Functional Heads, Finance and Administration of all Departments and Agencies

Subject: Replacement of the North American Free Trade Agreement

## Summary

This Contracting Policy Notice covers three elements: the replacement of NAFTA, the updated procurement thresholds for free trade agreements, and the collection of country of origin statistics.

1. Replacement of the North American Free Trade Agreement

On July 1, 2020, the Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement (CUSMA) came into force, replacing the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA). However, unlike NAFTA, Canada is not a party to the government procurement chapter in CUSMA, which pertains only to Mexico and the United States. Canada will maintain government procurement commitments with the United States through the World Trade Organization Agreement on Government Procurement and with Mexico through the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership.

NAFTA will not apply to any procurements that commence on or after July 1, 2020.

It is advised that departments apply the following instructions with respect to the NAFTA-CUSMA transition:

- If no solicitation documentation relating to the procurement is published before July 1, 2020, NAFTA will not apply to the procurement.
- If solicitation documentation relating to the procurement that will lead to award of an instrument is published before July 1, 2020, NAFTA will continue to apply to the procurement until it is complete.
- If only a Request for Information or information-gathering document is published before July 1, 2020, NAFTA will not apply to the solicitation itself.
- For existing standing offers put in place before July 1, 2020, the standing offer should simply be administered in accordance with its terms.
- For a procurement subject to NAFTA with a process involving a one-time prequalification list (e.g. Invitations to Qualify) where the prequalification documentation was published before July 1, 2020, NAFTA will continue to apply to that procurement until it is complete.
- For existing supply arrangements put in place before July 1, 2020:

The government procurement obligations of the other trade agreements to which the Government of Canada is a party will continue to apply. Entities are strongly advised to review the obligations of these agreements to ensure they account for any changes resulting from the rescission of NAFTA.

1. Collection of Country of Origin Statistics

Following the replacement of NAFTA, Canada will no longer collect procurement statistics concerning country of origin. For Government of Canada statistical reporting purposes, departments are now to report the country of the vendor’s address under the Country of Vendor data field that will replace the Country of Origin data field in proactive disclosure of contract reports.
